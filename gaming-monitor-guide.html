Essential Tips and Tricks

  • Always keep your GPU and CPU drivers updated to the latest stable version
  • Use Game Mode in Windows 11 to prioritize resources for your games
  • Monitor your CPU and GPU temperatures with MSI Afterburner to prevent thermal throttling
  • Install your games on an NVMe SSD for dramatically faster load times
  • Use NVIDIA DLSS or AMD FSR to boost frame rates without sacrificing much visual quality
  • Enable hardware-accelerated GPU scheduling in Windows for better latency
  • Keep your Windows installation clean with regular maintenance to ensure peak performance

Performance Optimization

Getting the best performance out of your PC involves more than just having powerful hardware. Software optimization is equally important. Start by ensuring your operating system is up to date and free of unnecessary background processes that can steal resources from your games. Regularly defragmenting your HDD (SSDs do not need this) and keeping your system clean from malware and bloatware can make a noticeable difference in gaming performance.

In-game settings have a huge impact on performance. Learning which settings have the biggest impact on frame rates — such as resolution scaling, shadow quality, and ambient occlusion — allows you to find the perfect balance between visual quality and performance for your specific hardware.
