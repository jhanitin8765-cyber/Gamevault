Release Date & Platforms

GTA 6 is Rockstar Games' most anticipated project in over a decade. After years of rumors and leaks, Rockstar officially confirmed the game with its first trailer in December 2023. The game is targeting a 2025 release window for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S, with PC following later.

Developer

💡 PC Players Note: GTA 6 will launch on consoles first. Based on Rockstar's history with GTA 5, a PC release may come 1-2 years after the console launch.

Story and Characters

GTA 6 features two protagonists — Jason and Lucia — making this the first GTA game to feature a playable female lead since the franchise's early days. Lucia is confirmed to be a criminal who teams up with Jason in what appears to be a Bonnie and Clyde style partnership operating in the criminal underworld of modern Vice City.

The trailer showed a world that feels genuinely alive — social media influencers filming themselves, viral moments happening in the background, and a satirical take on modern internet culture that is very much in line with GTA's tradition of social commentary.

Map and World

GTA 6 is set in Vice City and the surrounding state of Leonida — Rockstar's fictional take on Florida. The map appears to be the largest in GTA history, featuring not just the city of Vice City but also surrounding areas including the Everglades, smaller towns, and rural regions. The trailer hinted at genuine environmental diversity from dense urban areas to swamplands.

New Features Confirmed

  • Dual protagonist system — switch between Jason and Lucia
  • Most detailed NPC AI ever seen in a Rockstar game
  • Dynamic economy system affecting prices and opportunities
  • Improved wanted system with smarter law enforcement
  • Social media mechanic — NPCs can film and post about player actions
  • GTA Online will continue with major new features
  • Most realistic weather and environmental simulation yet
