Overview

DOOM: The Dark Ages is the prequel to DOOM (2016) and DOOM Eternal, taking the legendary franchise into an unexpected medieval fantasy setting. Developed by id Software, The Dark Ages promises to be the most epic and cinematic DOOM game yet while retaining the series' signature brutal, fast-paced combat.

New Setting and Story

Set thousands of years before the events of DOOM 2016, The Dark Ages takes the Doom Slayer to a medieval fantasy realm where demons have invaded a world of knights, castles, and dark magic. The visual contrast of hell's demons invading a medieval European setting creates a unique aesthetic that sets this game apart from anything id Software has done before.

Major Gameplay Changes

The Dark Ages introduces significant gameplay changes that distinguish it from its predecessors. The game is deliberately slower and more grounded than DOOM Eternal, focusing on shield-based combat, devastating new melee attacks, and a power fantasy of being an unstoppable juggernaut rather than a nimble speed-runner.

  • New Shield — parry projectiles, bash enemies, and use as a weapon
  • Atlan Mech — pilot a giant mechanized suit in large-scale battles
  • Dragon — fly a dragon in aerial combat sequences
  • New weapons including a flail and spike launcher
  • More deliberate, weighty combat versus Eternal's speed
  • Larger scale battles with hundreds of enemies on screen
  • Mick Gordon returns to compose the soundtrack
