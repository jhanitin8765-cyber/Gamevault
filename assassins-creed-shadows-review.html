Overview

Assassin's Creed Shadows is the entry fans have been requesting for over a decade — a full Assassin's Creed game set in feudal Japan. After years of speculation and a tumultuous development period, Ubisoft has finally delivered their Japanese AC experience. The result is a game with moments of genuine brilliance alongside familiar Ubisoft open world fatigue.

Story

Set in 16th century Japan during the Sengoku period, Shadows tells the story of Naoe, a young shinobi seeking revenge, and Yasuke, the historical African samurai who served under Oda Nobunaga. The two protagonists cross paths and eventually work together against a shadowy organization threatening to destabilize Japan.

Dual Protagonist Gameplay

The dual protagonist system is Shadows' greatest strength. Naoe plays like a traditional Assassin's Creed character — agile, stealthy, and focused on assassination. Yasuke is a completely different experience — slower but devastating, with heavy samurai combat that feels more like a character action game.

  • Switch between Naoe and Yasuke at most points in the game
  • Each protagonist has unique skill trees and abilities
  • Stealth missions favour Naoe, combat challenges favour Yasuke
  • Seasonal weather system changes world appearance and gameplay
  • Japanese architecture climbing feels fantastic
  • Grappling hook returns for vertical traversal

World Design

Feudal Japan is a gorgeous setting for an Assassin's Creed game. Cherry blossoms, snow-capped mountains, dense bamboo forests, and bustling castle towns create a visually diverse world that is a joy to explore. The seasonal system dynamically changes the world's appearance throughout the year.

✅ Great — The Best AC in Years

Assassin's Creed Shadows delivers the feudal Japan experience fans have been wanting. The dual protagonist system is inspired, the world is beautiful, and the combat is satisfying. Open world bloat holds it back from greatness.
