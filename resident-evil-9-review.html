Overview

Resident Evil 9 is Capcom's most ambitious entry in the long-running survival horror franchise. Building on the incredible foundations of RE7, RE8 Village, and the phenomenal RE4 Remake, RE9 delivers a horrifying, exhilarating, and emotionally resonant experience that stands as the definitive achievement of the modern Resident Evil era.

We played through the game twice — once on Standard difficulty and once on Hardcore — spending over 30 hours in total with the game before writing this review. What Capcom has created here is nothing short of extraordinary.

💡 Quick Verdict: Resident Evil 9 is a masterclass in survival horror game design. If you are a fan of the series or horror games in general, this is an absolutely essential purchase.

Story and Setting

Without spoiling the plot, Resident Evil 9 takes the series in a bold new direction while honoring its roots. The game opens in a remote coastal European town gripped by a mysterious outbreak that transforms its inhabitants into grotesque creatures unlike anything seen in previous entries. The atmosphere is oppressive and haunting from the very first moments.

The story weaves together threads from across the series' history in ways that will deeply satisfy longtime fans while remaining accessible to newcomers. The writing is the strongest in the franchise, with characters that feel genuinely human and motivations that are actually believable. The villain of RE9 is one of gaming's most memorable antagonists in recent years.

The game runs approximately 15-20 hours for a first playthrough, which is substantial for a survival horror game. The pacing is excellent — the game rarely outstays its welcome and consistently introduces new mechanics and environments to keep the experience fresh.

😱 Scare Factor Rating

Resident Evil 9 is genuinely terrifying in its early hours. Jump scares are used sparingly and effectively, but the true horror comes from sustained tension and incredible creature design. This is not a game to play alone at midnight.

Gameplay and Combat

RE9 continues the over-the-shoulder third-person perspective of the mainline modern entries while introducing significant gameplay innovations. The new Adaptive Arsenal system allows you to modify weapons on the fly using materials found in the environment, creating builds that suit your playstyle.

Resource management is more important than ever. Ammo is scarce on Standard difficulty and genuinely precious on Hardcore. Every shot counts, and learning to use the environment — explosive barrels, traps, and enemy behavior — is crucial to surviving the game's more intense sequences.

The dodge and parry system from RE4 Remake returns and has been refined significantly. Perfectly timed parries open up brutal counterattack animations that are incredibly satisfying to pull off. The combat feels weighty and impactful in a way that makes every encounter meaningful.

New Gameplay Features

  • Adaptive Arsenal: Real-time weapon modification system with dozens of upgrade paths
  • Environmental Kills: New interactive hazards that can be used against enemies creatively
  • Companion AI: Your partner character is genuinely useful and never feels like a burden
  • Sanity System: Prolonged stress affects your character visually and mechanically
  • New Game Plus: Unlocks additional weapons, costumes, and a remixed enemy layout

Horror Elements

RE9 is the scariest Resident Evil game since RE7. The enemy design is nightmarish — Capcom's artists have created creatures that are deeply unsettling in their design, movement, and sound design. The first major enemy type you encounter is introduced in a sequence that will be discussed for years in horror gaming circles.

The sound design deserves special recognition. The game uses silence and ambient noise masterfully, building tension in quiet moments before shattering it with startling encounters. Playing with headphones is strongly recommended — the spatial audio creates an immersive horror experience that genuinely makes you feel the world around you.

The level design returns to the more linear but densely packed environments of classic Resident Evil while incorporating modern exploration elements. Secret areas reward thorough searching with lore documents, upgrade materials, and hidden treasures. The game always feels designed rather than procedurally generated.

Graphics and PC Performance

Running on an updated version of Capcom's RE Engine, Resident Evil 9 is one of the best-looking games available on PC. Character models are photorealistic, environments are incredibly detailed, and the lighting system creates genuine atmosphere that enhances the horror experience.

PC performance is excellent. Capcom has consistently delivered well-optimized PC ports in recent years and RE9 continues that tradition. On a mid-range system with an RTX 3060 and Ryzen 5 5600, the game runs at a locked 60fps at 1080p High settings. On higher-end hardware, 4K 60fps is achievable without significant compromises.

The game supports NVIDIA DLSS 3, AMD FSR 3, and Intel XeSS. Ray tracing is available and dramatically improves the quality of reflections and shadows, though it is demanding and best reserved for RTX 4070 and above.

Final Verdict

Resident Evil 9 is a landmark achievement in survival horror. Capcom has delivered a game that honors the franchise's legacy while pushing it boldly into new territory. With its exceptional story, refined combat, nightmarish horror, and stunning presentation, RE9 is not just the best Resident Evil game ever made — it is one of the greatest horror games in the history of the medium.
