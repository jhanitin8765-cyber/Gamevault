Mafia: The Old Country –
Preview & What We Know

📅 March 2025✍️ Alex Storm⏱ 7 min read
📅 Release: 2025

Overview

Mafia: The Old Country is the fourth mainline entry in 2K Games' beloved crime drama franchise. Set in early 20th century Sicily, Italy, this prequel takes the series back to its roots — exploring the origins of the Sicilian Mafia in a period-accurate setting that promises to be both visually stunning and narratively rich.

💡 Key Fact: Mafia: The Old Country is set before any previous Mafia game, exploring the very origins of the criminal organizations seen in the original trilogy.

Setting and Story

The game is set in the sun-drenched hills and coastal towns of early 20th century Sicily. Players take on the role of Enzo Favara, a young man born into poverty who becomes entangled with the Sicilian Mafia through desperate circumstances. The story explores themes of loyalty, honor, and the corrupting influence of power in a world where survival often depends on ruthlessness.

Hangar 13 has confirmed that The Old Country will be a more focused, narrative-driven experience compared to Mafia 3's open world design. The game takes clear inspiration from the original Mafia's linear, story-first approach which was praised by fans of the series.

Gameplay Changes

  • Set in period-accurate early 1900s Sicily — no modern vehicles or weapons
  • Horse-drawn carriages as primary transportation
  • Period-appropriate firearms — revolvers, early shotguns
  • Strong focus on melee combat and stealth
  • Linear chapter-based structure inspired by original Mafia
  • No open world — more curated, handcrafted environments
